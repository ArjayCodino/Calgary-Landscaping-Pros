Introduction to landscaping

Landscaping is the art and science of designing and maintaining outdoor spaces, including gardens, lawns, trees, shrubs, and other vegetation. It involves the use of various elements such as plants, water features, and hardscaping to create functional and visually appealing outdoor environments. In Calgary, AB, landscaping plays a significant role in enhancing the beauty and functionality of residential and commercial properties.

With its harsh climate and unique terrain, Calgary presents both challenges and opportunities for landscaping. The city's short growing season, dry conditions, and extreme weather require careful planning and maintenance to ensure successful landscaping projects. However, the region's natural beauty and diverse plant life offer a wealth of inspiration and possibilities for creative landscaping design. Whether you are looking to create a beautiful garden oasis in your backyard or improve the curb appeal of your business, landscaping in Calgary offers endless possibilities for creating stunning outdoor spaces.


Types of landscaping

Landscaping can be broadly categorized into several different types, each with its own unique characteristics and design considerations. Here are some of the most common types of landscaping:


  • Residential Landscaping - This type of landscaping is designed for private homes and includes elements such as lawns, gardens, trees, and hardscaping features such as patios and pathways.

  • Commercial Landscaping - Commercial landscaping is designed for businesses and public spaces, such as parks, office buildings, and shopping centers. This type of landscaping often includes larger, more complex designs, and may incorporate features such as fountains, sculptures, and outdoor seating areas.
  • Xeriscaping - Xeriscaping is a type of landscaping that is designed to conserve water by using drought-tolerant plants and reducing the need for irrigation. This type of landscaping is especially popular in arid regions like Calgary.

  • Ecological Landscaping - Ecological landscaping focuses on creating outdoor spaces that are environmentally sustainable and promote biodiversity. This may include the use of native plants, natural water features, and other elements that support local ecosystems.

  • Urban Landscaping - Urban landscaping is designed to create outdoor spaces in densely populated areas, such as cities and towns. This type of landscaping often involves the use of small, compact designs that maximize space and incorporate elements such as green roofs and vertical gardens.

  • Sports and Recreational Landscaping - This type of landscaping is designed for athletic fields, golf courses, and other outdoor recreational spaces. It often includes features such as turf grass, irrigation systems, and specialized lighting for night games.
